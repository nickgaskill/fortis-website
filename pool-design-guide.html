Design your pool in six choices

A custom pool sounds like a thousand decisions. It's really six. This guide walks you through each one, and your Fortis designer handles everything in between.

  1. Choose your style
  2. Pick a size
  3. Add a spa
  4. Pick your extras
  5. Select your finish & tile
  6. We build it — start to finish

Dog-ear your favorites and bring them to your free consultation. There are no wrong answers — every Fortis pool is designed around your yard, your family, and your budget.

02

Step one

Choose your style

Photo — geometric pool

Geometric

Clean lines and crisp corners — rectangles, lap pools, and modern edges that mirror the architecture of the house.

Photo — modern freeform pool

Modern Freeform

Soft curves with a contemporary feel — organic shapes that flow with the landscape instead of against it.

Photo — custom pool

Completely Custom

If you can sketch it, we can build it — vanishing edges, tanning ledges, swim-up bars, and one-of-a-kind shapes.

03

Step two

Pick a size

Four starting points — every one is adjusted to fit your yard and how you'll actually use it.

Petite~ 10 × 20 ft

Plunge and cocktail pools — big impact in a compact yard, easy to heat and chill.

Classic~ 14 × 28 ft

The family standard — room to swim, play, and gather without giving up the whole yard.

Grand~ 16 × 36 ft

Serious swimming and serious entertaining — space for laps, games, and a crowd.

Imperial18 × 40 ft +

The estate pool — resort scale, statement features, and room for everything.

Footprints are typical starting points, not limits — your site visit sets the real dimensions.

04

Step three

Add a spa

Photo — raised spa with spillover

Integrated spa

Built into the pool's shape and finished to match — one seamless body of water.

Raised spa with spillover

Elevated above the waterline with a cascading spill — the classic centerpiece look and sound.

Detached spa

Its own vessel, placed where you want it — closer to the house, the view, or the fire feature.

All spas include therapy jets, bench seating, and fast independent heating for year-round use.

05

Step four

Pick your extras

The details that make it yours — add any of these to your build.

Fountains & water features

Sheer descents, bubblers, scuppers, and deck jets.

Salt water system

Softer water, gentler on skin and eyes, less daily chemistry.

UV sanitation

An extra layer of clean that cuts chemical demand.

Heater

Swim from spring to Thanksgiving, not just July.

Chiller

Because Texas August water should still feel refreshing.

Auto-fill

The pool tops itself off — no hose, no guesswork.

Robotic cleaner

Walls and floor scrubbed while you're at work.

Tree protection

Root barriers and smart placement that protect your oaks — and your shell.

06

Step five · finishes

Gunite & plaster

Every Fortis pool is gunite — steel-reinforced concrete, shaped on site and built to last decades. Your plaster finish sets the color of the water itself.

Pure White · bright azure water Ivory · warm tropical blue Cool Grey · crisp light blue French Grey · deep sky blue Graphite · lagoon blue Caribbean Aqua · turquoise Tahoe Blue · sapphire Midnight · reflective lake

Representative colors — pebble and quartz upgrades available. Physical samples at your consultation.
